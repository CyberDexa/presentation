"Social engineering isn't about hacking computers - it's about hacking humans. These criminals are master manipulators who study human psychology."

"They know exactly which buttons to push: fear ('Your account will be closed!'), greed ('You've won money!'), urgency ('Act now!'), and trust ('I'm your friend's cousin')."

üí∞ Instagram Giveaway Scam

Scenario: You see a post from "@NikeOfficial" saying: "WIN A FREE PAIR OF AIR JORDANS! Just DM us your email and we'll send you the entry form!"

What do you do?

üö® DANGER! This is a scam!

Why it's suspicious:

  • ‚úÖ Real companies don't ask for personal info via DM
  • ‚úÖ Legitimate giveaways use official websites or apps
  • ‚úÖ Check the account - is it verified? (blue checkmark)

Safe response: Visit nike.com directly or use their official app for legitimate giveaways.

üì± "Your Account is Suspended"

Scenario: You get a text: "Your Netflix account has been suspended due to suspicious activity. Click here to verify: [suspicious-link]."

What do you do?

üö® DANGER! Phishing attempt!

Why it's suspicious:

  • ‚úÖ Netflix never sends account suspension texts
  • ‚úÖ They communicate through their app/website only
  • ‚úÖ URL is suspicious (hover to check)

Safe response: Open Netflix app directly and check your account status there.

üéÆ Gaming Friend Request

Scenario: Someone adds you on Discord claiming to be a friend from your favorite game. They say: "Hey! Remember me from Fortnite? Let's team up!"

What do you do?

ü§î CAUTION! Could be legitimate, but verify first!

How to safely respond:

  • ‚úÖ Ask specific questions only you and your real friend would know
  • ‚úÖ Don't share personal information
  • ‚úÖ If unsure, politely decline and suggest connecting through the game

Remember: Scammers often pretend to be gamers to build trust and ask for money or account details later.

üìß "Package Delivery Failed"

Scenario: Email from "Amazon Delivery": "Your package couldn't be delivered. Pay $2.99 re-delivery fee here: [link]"

What do you do?

üö® DANGER! Classic phishing scam!

Why it's suspicious:

  • ‚úÖ Amazon never asks for payment via email links
  • ‚úÖ Check sender email - real Amazon emails end in @amazon.com
  • ‚úÖ Small amounts ($2.99) are used to lower suspicion

Safe response: Track your package through the official Amazon app or website.

üì∏ Social Media "Hack" Claim

Scenario: Friend messages you: "OMG someone hacked my Instagram! Can you send me $50 for a new phone? I'll pay you back tomorrow!"

What do you do?

üö® DANGER! Account takeover scam!

Why it's suspicious:

  • ‚úÖ Real friends would call or use another method to verify
  • ‚úÖ Scammers hack accounts and message friends for money
  • ‚úÖ "Pay me back tomorrow" is a common promise

Safe response: Call your friend using their known phone number (not the one they messaged from) to verify.

üí≥ "Security Update Required"

Scenario: Bank app notification: "Security Update Required. Click here to update your information or your account will be locked."

What do you do?

ü§î CAUTION! Could be legitimate, but verify!

How to safely respond:

  • ‚úÖ Open your bank app directly (don't click links)
  • ‚úÖ Check for official notifications within the app
  • ‚úÖ Call your bank using the number on your card (not from the message)

Remember: Real banks give you time to update information and never threaten immediate account closure.

üîç Before You Click

  • Hover over links to see the real URL
  • Check for spelling mistakes in domains
  • Look for HTTPS and the padlock icon
  • Be suspicious of urgent language

üì± Social Media Safety

  • Verify news from multiple sources
  • Check the publication date
  • Look for author credentials
  • Question emotional content

üîê Account Security

  • Use unique passwords for each account
  • Enable two-factor authentication
  • Never share login details
  • Log out of shared computers

ü§î Critical Thinking

  • Look for evidence and citations
  • Trust your instincts
